Environmental Requirements — Marine and Mobile
Section 6 — Marine & Mobile Applications
Ingress protection minimum
IP54
Marine Machinery Space IP Rating
IP44 is absolute minimum. IP54 (dust protected + splash) is required for engine rooms and open deck machinery spaces on commercial vessels. E-frac outdoor equipment: IP54 minimum; consider IP65 for coastal / offshore wellsites.
Temperature range — e-frac mobile
−40 / +70°C
Ambient Operating Range
E-frac trailers must operate in winter Permian Basin / Bakken conditions (−40 °C ambient start) and summer desert heat (+70 °C inside an unventilated trailer). Drive and motor specification must cover the full range without derating.
Vibration test standard
IEC 60068-2-6
Sinusoidal Vibration Type Test
Marine classification societies and e-frac operators require equipment to be type-tested to IEC 60068-2-6 (or equivalent) sinusoidal vibration test. Road transport vibration profile (5–50 Hz, 2g) must also be confirmed — separate from operational vibration.
Salt-fog/humidity test
IEC 60068-2-52
Salt Mist / Corrosion Type Test
Cyclic salt-mist testing required for marine equipment. Class societies (ABS, DNV, BV) require 5% NaCl salt mist type-test per IEC 60068-2-52 or equivalent. All connectors, glands, and enclosures must survive 48–96 hour salt spray without insulation degradation.
Marine Environmental Checklist
Humidity: IEC 60068-2-78 — 40 °C / 93% RH cyclic damp heat. Marine engine rooms regularly operate at > 85% RH during port calls in tropical regions. All PCB assemblies in the drive should be conformally coated.
Shock: IEC 60068-2-27 — 15g peak, 11 ms half-sine. Required for coastal vessels subject to wave impact and for any equipment installed above the waterline on push boats operating in choppy river conditions.
Classification society type-approval: ABS, DNV, LR, or BV type-approval is required if the vessel is classed. Verify the drive manufacturer's type-approval certificate covers the specific drive model, power rating, and IP rating specified.
Cable insulation: Marine cables must be LSZH (Low Smoke Zero Halogen) and fire-retardant per IEC 60332. Standard PVC cables from industrial applications are not acceptable on classified vessels.
E-Frac Mobile Equipment Specifics
Flex-rated cables: All motor cables on mobile trailers must be flex-rated (IEC 60245 or equivalent) — standard fixed-installation cables fail from fatigue cracking after repeated trailer moves. Specify minimum bend radius ≥ 6× cable diameter at trailer cable entry glands.
Trailer vibration: Drive enclosure mounting must account for road transport vibration. Use vibration-isolating mounts on the drive cabinet legs. Check manufacturer vibration survival spec — not all standard drives are rated for transport on flatbed trailers at highway speed.
IEEE 519 — harmonic distortion: At the wellsite generator bus, current harmonic distortion (THD-I) must not exceed 5% (IEEE 519:2022 limits for < 1 kV systems). Active front-end (AFE) drives or passive 12-pulse rectifiers are required at large pump spreads to meet this limit.
Key Concept
Marine and mobile environments impose environmental requirements that go well beyond standard industrial specifications. Minimum requirements: IP54 enclosures, IEC 60068-2-52 salt mist, IEC 60068-2-6 vibration, −40 °C to +70 °C temperature range for e-frac. Verify classification society type-approval for classed vessels. Use LSZH cables on marine vessels.
