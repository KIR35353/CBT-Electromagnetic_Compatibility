Practical Installation Measures
Section 3 — Installation & Filters
Drive Cabinet — Key EMC Installation Points
A — Backplate Metal Contact
Drive mounting flanges must make direct, unpainted metal-to-metal contact with the backplate. Use a wire brush or flap disc to remove paint. The drive chassis is the primary HF ground reference — not the PE wire.
B — 360° EMC Cable Glands
Metallic cable glands with full circumferential contact strip the cable jacket and clamp around the shield braid at the entry plate. The entry plate must also be metal and bonded to the backplate. Plastic glands are not acceptable.
C — PE and Equipotential Bond
Connect drive PE terminal to the panel PE busbar. Add a ≥ 10 mm² equipotential conductor from the drive chassis to the motor frame. This limits the voltage difference between the two endpoints of the CM current path.
D — Cable Segregation
Keep shielded motor cables ≥ 300 mm from control and signal cables. Where they must cross, ensure a 90°crossing angle. Never bundle motor and signal cables in the same conduit or trunking.
Key Concept
The four critical installation measures are: (A) bare-metal backplate contact, (B) 360° EMC cable glands, (C) PE busbar and equipotential bond, (D) power/signal cable segregation. All four must be present — any single omission can cause compliance failure.
