Common-Mode Output Filters
Section 3 — Installation & Filters
CM Filter — Nanocrystalline Core Topology
What a CM Filter Addresses
A CM output filter reduces the common-mode current flowing from the inverter output through parasitic motor-frame capacitance. This directly reduces shaft voltage and bearing current — the cause of EDM bearing failure in motors > 75 kW.
Nanocrystalline Cores
High-permeability nanocrystalline alloy toroids present very high impedance to CM current (typically 50–500 Ω at 10 kHz) while presenting near-zero impedance to differential-mode load current. Only CM current is attenuated — motor performance is unchanged.
Critical Installation Rule
All three phase conductors must pass through the core. If any phase is omitted, or if the PE conductor or cable shield passes through the core, the DM cancellation is disrupted and the filter will not work correctly. Follow the manufacturer's wiring diagram exactly.
When to Use vs. Shaft Brush
For motors > 75 kW: CM output filter is preferred. For smaller motors: a shaft grounding brush is lower cost. On marine propulsion drives, shaft grounding brushes serve double duty — they also prevent stray current corrosion of bronze propellers and sea chests.
Key Concept
CM output filters use nanocrystalline toroids to present high impedance to common-mode current while passing differential (motor) current unchanged. All three phases must pass through the core. CM filters reduce bearing voltage and are used alongside shaft grounding brushes for large motors on marine propulsion drives.
