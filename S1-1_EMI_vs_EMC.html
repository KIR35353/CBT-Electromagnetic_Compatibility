EMI vs. EMC — Two Sides of the Same Problem
Section 1 — What Is EMC?
Concept: A device must be controlled in emission (what it puts out) and adequate in immunity (what it can tolerate). The gap between these two levels is the compatibility margin — the wider it is, the safer the installation.
🔴 Emission — What the Device Outputs
▶
Definition: Electromagnetic energy unintentionally radiated or conducted by a device into its environment.
▶
AC Drive Source: The inverter's high-frequency PWM switching generates common-mode and differential-mode conducted noise on the supply leads and radiated emissions from motor cables.
▶
Regulatory Limit: EN 55011 / CISPR 11 defines conducted and radiated emission limits by environment class. Drives must meet these limits — tested as a complete system including motor cable.
▶
Controlled By: RFI filter on mains input, shielded motor cable, 360° shield termination, and drive-to-backplate metal bonding.
vs
🟢 Immunity — What the Device Can Tolerate
▶
Definition: The ability of a device to continue operating correctly when exposed to electromagnetic disturbances from its environment.
▶
Tested Against: IEC 61000-4 series — electrostatic discharge (ESD), radiated and conducted RF, fast transients, surges, voltage dips, and magnetic fields.
▶
Performance Criteria: Class A = operates normally during test. Class B = temporary degradation, self-recovers. Class C = requires reset but no data loss. Drives must meet Class A for most industrial immunity tests.
▶
Marine Relevance: On a vessel, immunity matters — the drive must not misoperate due to the radio transmitters, radar, or lightning surges present in the marine electromagnetic environment.
Compatibility Margin: The gap between the victim device's immunity level and the source device's emission level. A positive margin means the system coexists correctly. The wider the margin, the more robust the installation against unexpected disturbances.
Key Concept
EMC requires both controlled emission and adequate immunity. An AC drive installation must meet published emission limits AND ensure that the drive itself, and connected equipment (AIS, VHF radio, GPS), are not degraded by the drive's electromagnetic output.
