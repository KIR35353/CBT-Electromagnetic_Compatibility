Bearing Electric Stress — Key Numbers
Section 4 — Bearing Electric Stress
Lubricant film thickness
0.5μm
Bearing Oil Film Thickness
The Hertzian contact zone — thinner than a human hair. This thin film separates the rolling element from the race. At this thickness, dielectric breakdown occurs at very low voltages.
EDM threshold voltage
~10V
Bearing Breakdown Voltage
At ~10 V across the 0.5 μm film, the lubricant's dielectric strength is exceeded. The film punctures and a current discharge occurs — electroerosion of the metal surface.
Typical shaft voltage
10–50V
Drive-Induced Shaft Voltage
The CM voltage produced by a modern inverter-driven motor creates a capacitively-coupled shaft voltage. Without mitigation, this routinely exceeds the 10 V EDM threshold.
Motor size risk threshold
75kW
Minimum Risk — Shaft Grounding
Motors > 75 kW have sufficient rotor-to-stator capacitance to develop damaging shaft voltages in most VSD applications. Mitigation is mandatory above this size.

Why EDM Matters in Marine Applications
!
Propulsion bearing EDM = mission-critical failure. A propulsion motor bearing failure on a towboat pushing laden tank barges is not a maintenance event — it is a safety emergency. 46 CFR §143.585 requires single-fault tolerance for propulsion control.
!
Shaft grounding brushes on marine propulsion motors provide double protection: they reduce bearing EDM risk AND provide a low-impedance path for stray CM current — preventing stray current corrosion of bronze propellers and sea chests.
✓
Maintenance interval: Bearings in drives or motors operating in continuous marine service should be inspected every 2,000–4,000 hours or at annual dry-dock — whichever comes first — regardless of EDM mitigation.
Key Concept
The bearing oil film is only 0.5 μm thick and breaks down at ~10 V. Drive-induced shaft voltages of 10–50 V routinely exceed this threshold. For motors > 75 kW, EDM mitigation is mandatory — especially on marine propulsion systems where bearing failure has direct safety consequences.
