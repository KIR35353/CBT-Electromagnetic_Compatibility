Introduction · Screen 2 of 4
Who This Course Is For
This course supports marine vessel designers, mobile equipment engineers, and drive system integrators involved with AC drive specification, installation, and commissioning in safety-critical applications.
Applications focus on USCG Subchapter M towing vessels (inland waterways) and mobile electric fracturing (e-frac) equipment — where drive faults are not merely operational events but safety emergencies.
A sound understanding of EMC enables correct installation decisions for these environments: hull corrosion prevention, ungrounded distribution system compatibility, and regulatory compliance under 46 CFR Subchapter M and NEC.

Course Application
◉
Primary contexts:
Subchapter M inland towboats (USCG-inspected) and mobile electric fracturing equipment — applications where drive reliability directly determines vessel safety and well-site safety.
◉
Applicable frameworks:
46 CFR Part 143 (machinery & electrical), ABYC E-11, ABS Rivers & Intracoastal Rules, IEC 60533, NFPA 70 NEC, IEEE 519.
◉
Source material:
Danfoss Motor and Drive Text, Chapter 6 — Electromagnetic Compatibility.
Principles apply across all major drive manufacturers.
⚡ On a towing vessel or e-frac unit, drive EMC failures affect navigation safety, hull integrity, and well-site operations.
!
Every installation decision — cable type, grounding strategy, filter selection — either manages or worsens the EMC behaviour of the system.

Learning Objectives
1
Section 1
Explain the concepts of EMI and EMC, and identify the four interference coupling mechanisms.
2
Section 2
Apply correct grounding and shielding techniques, including proper 360° shield termination and ground-loop prevention.
3
Section 3
Select appropriate output filters (du/dt, sine-wave, common-mode) based on application requirements.
4
Section 4
Identify the causes of electrical bearing stress (EDM) and apply the relevant mechanical and electrical mitigation measures.
5
Section 5
Identify applicable EMC standards for US marine and oilfield applications, including 46 CFR §143.575, IEC 60533, and the ABS Rivers and Intracoastal Waterways rules.
6
Section 6
Apply marine and mobile specific design requirements: IT system compatibility, stray current corrosion prevention, and drive fault response planning.
