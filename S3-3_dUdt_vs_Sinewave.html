du/dt Filters vs. Sine-Wave Filters
Section 3 — Installation & Filters
Decision Point: Both filter types protect motor winding insulation from drive voltage stress. The choice depends on cable length, motor age, and whether acoustic noise or multi-motor configurations are involved.
▶ du/dt Filter — Slew Rate Limiter
■
What it does: Reduces the rate of voltage rise at the drive output to below 500 V/μs using a series LC circuit tuned above the switching frequency.
■
Output waveform: Still PWM — the pulses are slower-edged, not sinusoidal. Motor current is essentially the same as without the filter.
✓
Best for: Cables 50–150 m. Standard inverter-duty motors. Protection against winding insulation stress only. Lowest insertion loss (~0.5% at full load).
!
Does not: Eliminate acoustic switching noise. Protect old non-inverter-duty motors. Significantly reduce bearing CM current.
■
Typical cost: 3–8% of drive cost. Compact, low loss. Can be mounted on drive or DIN rail.
vs
▶ Sine-Wave Filter — Full Waveform Conversion
■
What it does: LC filter tuned below the switching frequency — converts the PWM output to a near-sinusoidal current waveform. Peak voltage at motor terminals ≈ drive output voltage.
■
Output waveform: Sinusoidal current in the motor. Motor runs as if fed from a variable-frequency sine supply. Bearing current and acoustic noise are both significantly reduced.
✓
Best for: Cables > 150 m. Rewound motors or motors with non-inverter-rated insulation. Multiple motors on one drive output. Environments where acoustic noise is unacceptable.
!
Trade-offs: Higher insertion loss (~1.5–2% at full load). Physical size — requires dedicated cabinet space. More expensive (8–20% of drive cost).
■
Maximum switching frequency: Must match the drive's carrier frequency setting. Check filter specification — most rated at 4 kHz, some at 2 kHz only.
Key Concept
Use a du/dt filter when the problem is motor winding stress on cables > 50 m with a standard inverter-duty motor. Use a sine-wave filter when the motor is old or non-inverter-duty, cables exceed 150 m, acoustic noise is a concern, or multiple motors share a drive output.
