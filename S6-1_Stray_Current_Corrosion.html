Stray Current Corrosion
Section 6 — Marine & Mobile Applications
Stray CM Current Path — Towboat Propulsion Drive
The Mechanism
Drive Y-filter capacitors conduct CM current to the motor frame (PE). On a vessel, the motor frame is bonded to the steel hull. CM current that reaches the hull can leak into the conductive water as a stray current. At the propeller — a bronze/brass dissimilar metal — the stray current is an electrochemical cell that dissolves metal. This is galvanic corrosion accelerated by stray current.
Corrosion Rate
Approximately 9 kg of steel dissolved per ampere per year (Faraday's law). Even milliampere-level stray currents cause measurable corrosion over a season. Bronze propellers are common anodes in this electrochemical cell — they can be attacked faster than the zinc sacrificial anodes designed for the purpose.
46 CFR §143.570(f)
§143.570(f) prohibits connecting the vessel's distribution system neutral or ground to the hull (IT system) specifically to prevent stray current leakage. VSD Y-capacitors that connect to PE and therefore to the hull violate this provision. See Section 6-2 for the three engineering solutions to this conflict.
Engineering Controls
• 360° shield terminations keep CM current in the cable shield, not in the hull
• Shaft grounding brushes provide a low-impedance return path via the shaft to the drive — bypassing the water path
• Galvanic isolation transformer breaks the CM leakage path to the hull while allowing the drive to reference to a local PE
Key Concept
Drive CM current that reaches the steel hull of a metallic vessel can leak into the water as a stray current, causing electrochemical corrosion of bronze propellers, brass sea cocks, and aluminium heat exchangers. 46 CFR §143.570(f) prohibits hull-to-ground connections. Mitigation: 360° shield termination, shaft grounding brushes, and isolation transformers where required.
