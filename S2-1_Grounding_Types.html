Grounding Configurations
Section 2 — Grounding & Shielding
Context: Three grounding topologies are used in electrical installations. The choice affects both safety performance and EMC behaviour. In AC drive systems, only one topology is recommended for the equipment panel.
△ Single-Point Series
▶
Topology: Equipment grounded in a chain — each unit connects to the PE of the previous. One wire runs from unit to unit to earth.
!
Impedance Problem: The last unit in the chain has the longest, highest-impedance path to earth. CM current from one drive can couple into others through the shared impedance.
✗
Not Recommended for AC drive panels. The shared PE conductor creates a common impedance that allows noise transfer between drives.
▶
Acceptable for: Low-frequency systems where individual circuit isolation is needed (e.g., instrumentation star grounding below 1 MHz).
△ Single-Point Parallel (Star)
▶
Topology: Each unit has its own direct PE connection back to a single earth reference point (the PE busbar). No shared impedance in the return path.
✓
Recommended for Drive Panels. Each drive has a separate, direct PE conductor — and the drive chassis contacts the backplate for high-frequency bonding.
▶
At Low Frequency: Works well. Each device has an independent fault-current path. No cross-coupling between drives through shared PE conductor impedance.
!
At High Frequency: The PE conductor still has inductance. At MHz frequencies, the cable shield bonding to the backplate is the dominant return path — not the PE conductor.
△ Multipoint (HF Grid)
▶
Topology: Equipment is bonded to a conductive reference plane (DIN rail, backplate, or chassis) at multiple points. The reference plane itself is the ground.
✓
Best for High Frequencies. The low-inductance backplate provides a very low-impedance ground reference at MHz frequencies. This is the EMC grounding strategy for AC drive cabinets.
▶
In Practice: The drive is bolted to an unpainted metal backplate (zinc-plated or chemically treated). The cable shield clamp contacts the backplate. The backplate is then connected to the PE busbar via the star topology above.
✓
Required for EMC compliance. EN/IEC 61800-3 test results assume multipoint bonding at the drive chassis. Single-point or series grounding invalidates the CE mark test data.
Key Concept
Use multipoint (HF grid) grounding for the AC drive panel — direct metal-to-metal bonding to an unpainted backplate at every mounting point. Star (single-point parallel) grounding is used for the overall PE system. Series grounding must never be used for drive installations.
