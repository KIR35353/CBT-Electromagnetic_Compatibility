Why Grounding Matters
Section 2 — Grounding & Shielding
TWO PURPOSES OF GROUNDING
⚫ Safety Grounding
Connects equipment metalwork to earth so that if insulation fails, the fault current flows through the PE conductor to trip the protective device — not through a person. Mandatory under every electrical code.
The PE conductor must be continuous, low-resistance, and rated for fault current. It is not a signal reference.
⚫ EMC / Functional Grounding
Provides a low-impedance, high-frequency return path for common-mode current — back to the drive's DC link. Without this path, CM current flows through the mains supply, causing interference.
Requires direct metal-to-metal bonding — paint must be removed. The impedance must be low at MHz frequencies, not just DC.
46 CFR §143.570 — IT System Requirement (Subchapter M Vessels): Metallic towing vessels must use an ungrounded (IT) distribution system with continuous ground-fault detection. The hull must not be used as a current-carrying conductor. This creates a conflict with drive Y-capacitors that connect to ground — addressed in Section 6.
GROUNDING REQUIREMENTS FOR AC DRIVES
■
Direct metal-to-metal contact with backplate. Remove paint from all mounting contact surfaces. Paint is an insulator at high frequency.
■
PE conductor from drive to main PE bar. Sized per code. Short and direct. Use braided strap in preference to single round conductor for lower HF impedance.
■
Motor cable shield bonded to drive chassis at both ends. 360° cable gland connection — not a pigtail. This is the EMC-purpose ground return path for common-mode current.
!
Equipotential bonding conductor (≥ 10 mm²). Connect drive chassis, motor frame, and panel earth to the same reference. Eliminates ground loops between equipment.
!
No hull as conductor (marine). 46 CFR §143.570(f) prohibits using the vessel hull as a return current conductor. The PE system must be self-contained within the vessel wiring — not hull-referenced.
Key Concept
Grounding serves two distinct purposes: safety (fault protection) and EMC (CM current return). On marine vessels, 46 CFR §143.570 requires an IT (ungrounded) distribution system — which conflicts with standard drive Y-cap grounding. Awareness of this conflict is essential before specifying a drive installation on any inspected vessel.
