When to Use Output Filters
Section 3 — Installation & Filters
Motor cable length trigger
50m
du/dt Filter Threshold
Motor cables longer than ~50 m create voltage reflection (overshoot). A du/dt filter limits slew rate to ≤ 500 V/μs and reduces peak voltage at motor terminals.
Old motor winding stress
500V/μs
Max du/dt for Standard Motors
Pre-2000 rewound motors and low-cost motors may have insulation not rated for modern IGBT switching. A sine-wave filter eliminates this risk entirely.
Bearing EDM threshold
0.5μm
Lubricant Film Thickness
The bearing lubricant film is ~0.5 μm thick. A CM voltage across this film causes dielectric breakdown at ~10 V — leading to electrical discharge machining (EDM). A CM filter addresses this.
Acoustic noise threshold
1kHz
Switching Below Audible Range
If the carrier frequency falls in the audible range (1–4 kHz), motor coil vibration produces an audible tone. A sine-wave filter eliminates the harmonic content that drives this vibration.
Filter Selection Guide
■
du/dt filter: Short cables, standard motors, winding insulation stress only. Lowest cost and insertion loss. Still leaves PWM ripple on motor current.
■
Sine-wave filter: Long cables (> 100 m), older rewound motors, acoustic noise complaints, multi-motor configurations on one drive output. Converts PWM to near-sinusoidal output.
■
CM output filter: Bearing failure protection for large motors (> 75 kW on a single drive). Used when shaft grounding brushes are not practical. Reduces bearing current by 90%.
✓
No filter needed: Short cable (< 20 m), modern inverter-duty motor, cable and motor from the same manufacturer's specified combination — check the drive's application guide.
Drive-Induced Secondary Effects
!
Motor winding insulation stress: Steep voltage edges cause voltage doubling at motor terminals due to cable impedance mismatch. Hazardous for motors not rated for inverter duty.
!
Bearing EDM (electrical discharge machining): Common-mode voltage appears across bearing lubricant film. At threshold voltage, film breaks down — causing pitting on bearing races. See Section 4.
!
Acoustic switching noise: Carrier frequency audible in motor coils when fSW < 4 kHz. Higher switching frequency or sine-wave filter resolves this.
!
Supply harmonics: Drive input rectifier draws pulsed current — creates 5th, 7th harmonics on the supply. See IEEE 519 for limits. Not addressed by output filters.
Key Concept
Match the filter to the problem. du/dt for winding insulation stress with cable > 50 m. Sine-wave for long cables, old motors, or acoustic noise. Common-mode for bearing EDM protection on large motors. Do not over-specify — each filter type adds cost, losses, and bulk.
