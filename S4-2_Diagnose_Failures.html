Diagnosing Bearing Failures
Section 4 — Bearing Electric Stress
Diagnose before treating: Most bearing failures are mechanical or lubrication-related — not electrical. Applying EDM mitigation without confirming the root cause wastes cost and misses the real problem. Inspect removed bearings to determine actual failure mode.
▶ Mechanical Bearing Failure
🔍
Visual appearance: Pitting, spalling, or scoring concentrated at load zone. Smooth pits in a broad area. Possible metal-to-metal contact wear from lubrication loss. Race surface has no regular pattern.
🔧
Common causes: Shaft misalignment, excessive radial/axial load, improper fit (tight bore → preload), vibration transmission from connected equipment, contamination ingress, lubrication degradation or incorrect grease.
!
Warning signs before failure: Rising operating temperature, increasing vibration (broadband), grease discolouration, periodic noise linked to rotation speed harmonics.
✓
Corrective actions: Align shaft (check both angular and offset), verify bearing selection for load, re-lubricate with correct quantity and grade of grease, inspect seals and ingress protection, verify coupling condition.
vs
▶ Electrical Bearing Failure — EDM
🔍
Visual appearance — Frosting: Dull, matte "frosted" finish across the entire race surface. Under magnification: uniform micro-pitting from individual spark craters. Not load-zone specific — affects the whole race circumference.
🔍
Visual appearance — Fluting: Regular washboard-like ridges across the race, perpendicular to the rolling direction. Ridges spaced at intervals related to the switching frequency / rotation speed. Distinctive and unmistakable once you have seen it.
!
Confirm with shaft voltage measurement: Use a high-impedance oscilloscope probe (min 100 MΩ) to measure shaft-to-ground voltage before bearing replacement. If Vshaft > 10 V peak, EDM is confirmed. Document for warranty or incident reporting.
🔧
Corrective actions: Install shaft grounding brush and/or CM output filter. For large motors: insulated non-drive-end bearing. Replace bearing — the existing damage cannot be reversed. Confirm Vshaft < 10 V after mitigation before returning to service.
Marine Context: After a bearing failure on a VSD-driven propulsion motor, 46 CFR §143.585 requires documentation that the cause is identified and that recurrence risk is addressed before returning the vessel to service. A frosted or fluted bearing race is forensic evidence for the incident report. Preserve the bearing in a labelled container.
Key Concept
Frosting (uniform micro-pitting) and fluting (regular ridges across the race) are the diagnostic signatures of EDM bearing failure. Neither pattern appears in mechanical failures. Always measure shaft voltage before and after mitigation. Treat the cause — not just the symptom.
