Cable Shielding Performance
Section 2 — Grounding & Shielding
Transfer Impedance vs. Shield Construction
Transfer Impedance (ZT)
The measure of shield effectiveness: the voltage appearing inside the shield due to a current outside it. Measured in mΩ/m. Lower ZT = better shield. A pigtail tail termination adds milliohms at low frequency and orders of magnitude more at high frequency.
Shield Types for Drives
Single copper braid (85%) — minimum acceptable; rises sharply with frequency. Double braid (98%) — significantly better; recommended. Foil + braid (lapped) — best shielding; 100% optical coverage; preferred for variable speed drive motor cables.
The Four-Conductor Requirement
Motor cables for variable speed drives must have 4 conductors: three phases plus a symmetrically arranged PE conductor. This provides a symmetric CM current path and enables correct 360° shield termination at both ends.
Pigtail = Null and Void
A pigtail connection of the shield (twisted wire to a terminal) increases ZT by 10–100× at MHz frequencies — negating the cable's entire shielding benefit. Always use 360° EMC cable glands at both drive and motor.
Key Concept
Use a 4-conductor shielded motor cable with ≥98% braid coverage (double braid or foil+braid). Terminate the shield with 360° EMC cable glands at both ends. A pigtail termination dramatically increases transfer impedance and defeats the shield's purpose.
